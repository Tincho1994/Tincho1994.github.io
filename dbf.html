Design Build Fly

Fall 2013 - Spring 2017
Cornell University
Ithaca, NY

Design Build Fly is a student run project team at Cornell that competes at an annual interscholastic radio-control aircraft design competition hosted by AIAA. As the name suggests we design, build, and test all our prototypes.

I served for three years on the structures and airframe subteam, leading the subteam in my third year. During that time I aided in the structural design of fuselage components applying principles of static analysis of structures and mechanics of materials to optomize for weight. I also spent extensive time using Solidworks to CAD components and was responsible for integration of the team's comprehensive CAD models for two years. The subteam was also responsible for addressing the mechanisms and structures required for special missions unique to each year. Mission challenges included dropping payloads, transporting and assembling a second aircraft, collapsing the plane to fit in a tube, and several other requirements that changed from year to year.

In my fourth and final year on the team I served as one of the two team leads. My responsibilities focused on running all hands meetings, facitilitating communication between sub-teams, aiding in integration projects, and making executive design decisions.
