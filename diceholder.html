RPG Dice Carrier

Winter 2019

Overview

I like to feel good about my hobbies so I took on this project to pull up to the D&D table with the nicest set of dice. then at least if I rolled terribly that night, I'd be doing so in style. I used the Shaper Origin (Handheld CNC) to machine out the walnut wood and opted for some inlayed neodymium magnets to hold the two halves together. The dice pockets and tray are lined with a textured imitation leather. After my first version I added the tray to the top half to be able to roll on and a groove on the bottom to stack and save on table space.

Process

In order to get a nice fit on the mating halves I cut the top to nominal, and while cutting the bottom groove, slowly added an offset and checked until I got the slip fit I wanted (I ended up using this same offset for subsequent versions and it held). I wanted a really nice finish on this product and some of the geometry was difficult to sand so I opted for rough machining passes to save time and then fine passes at a optomized feeds/ speeds to reduce chatter.

For the surface finish I sanded the exterior cosmetic surfaces from 150 to 400 grit sandpaper. I sanded with the two pieces together to make sure the transition was smooth. The whole piece was finished with boiled linseed oil.

To properly size the imitation leather I 3D printed templates for cutting. I printed these with an offset to account for print/ cutting tolerances and aim for a slight undersize to reduce creasing when inlaying. At this point I also inlayed my magnets. The blind holes were sized to allow for float. I filled these with a touch of epoxy and made sure the magnets were flush while curing by using metal that rested partially on the surface.
