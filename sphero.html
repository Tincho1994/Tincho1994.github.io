Custom Sphero SPRK Chassis

Summer 2017
Cornell Autonomous Systems Lab

Previous Next

Sphero SPRK robots move in a similar manner to hamsters in a hamster ball. Not only does this mean that the outer shell must be unconstrained to move but it also severely limits the mechanical power of the robot. These two traits drove the design constraints for the chassis.

The chassis were also manufactured for an entire swarm so cost, lead time, and manufacturability were also emphasized.

Low resolution and rapid prototyping were used to verify designs throughout the process. All prototypes were designed using SolidWorks. Laser cutting was instrumental in quickly iterating through designs and final manufacturing for the swarm.
