Vibration Diagnostic Tool for Machine Health

Summer 2016
Shaper Tools - San Francisco CA

This project was focused around the redesign of a cyclic testing rig for Shaper Tools. The machine is meant to help characterize performance characteristics such as power draw and RPM for handheld power tool routers. The existing design induced a load on the router through the means of magnetic induction with a magnet at the end of a shaft spinning above a metal slab.

The original design was made primarily from wood. The redesign focused on producing a more robust aluminum structure as well as an integrated enclosure. Parts were designed in SolidWorks and sent to be machined. Additionally, a system was devised using a high end cpu cooling system to cool the metallic plate as the router spun the magnet above it. A stepper motor was also incorporated into the design to raise and lower the metal plate and create variable loads for testing.
